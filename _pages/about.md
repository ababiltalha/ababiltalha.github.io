I am Ababil (Aa-baa-beel), and I have recently graduated from [CSE, BUET](https://cse.buet.ac.bd/). I am currently as a Software Engineer at [Synesis IT PLC](https://www.synesisit.com.bd/). My research interests are related to **Intelligent Transportation Systems**, **Cyber-Physical Systems**, and **Autonomous Vehicles**. I am aiming to pursue a PhD program in relevant fields to further delve and make significant contributions in these areas.

Most of my research is focused on intelligent transportation and relevant cyber-physical systems. Currently, I am involved in a research project supervised by [Dr. Golam Md. Muktadir](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=RDa4TOgAAAAJ&hl=en), [Dr. A. B. M. Alim Al Islam](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=K-AIPzQAAAAJ&hl=en) and [Dr. Mahmuda Naznin](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=dJx58AQAAAAJ&hl=en) where we are working on **evaluating generative pedestrian models in autonomous vehicle simulations**. We utilize behavior observed in real-world data to prepare metrics for assessing how realistic generative pedestrian models are. Data analysis, visualization, and statistical modeling are the primary tools used on available pedestrian trajectory datasets in order to obtain realistic and quantifiable metrics.

My undergraduate thesis project, supervised by [Dr. A. B. M. Alim Al Islam](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=K-AIPzQAAAAJ&hl=en), was on developing an **intelligent traffic signaling system suitable for the non-lane-based heterogeneous traffic** of Dhaka, Bangladesh. The traffic in Dhaka, and similar cities of developing countries, is a disorganized collection of motorized and non-motorized vehicles with no regards to lanes. We propose a system architecture for an intelligent traffic signaling system catered to such urban traffic. To understand its feasibility and effectiveness in handling various traffic situations, the architecture was implemented and tested in a simulated environment indoors and in real-life traffic as well. We worked with realtime video processing, object detection and multi-objective optimization in edge devices, and experimented with our proposed pipeline as a working prototype. Prior to the development of the mentioned system architecture in our thesis work, I also contributed in preparing a traffic image dataset encapsulating the non-lane-based heterogeneous traffic of Dhaka city. The dataset was then used to train the object detection model in our intelligent traffic signaling system.
